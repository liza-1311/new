The Origins Of The Technique Are Murky, But What We Do Know Is That It Has Transcended Countries, Centuries And Civilisations, With Evidence Of It In The Embellished Furniture Of Ancient Egypt, Lacquerware Of 18Th Century Japan, And The Architecture Erected During India’s Mughal Empire.

Embedding Delicate Pieces Of Bone.

It involves placing meticulously carved, ethically sourced bone pieces into depressions created in wood.

Hand-carved Pieces Of Bone Into The Surface Of An Object.

The process involves shaping, cleaning, and painting, followed by creating complex floral, geometric, or mosaic patterns, usually finished with a coloured resin filler for a polished, contrasting effect.

In India, Mother Of Pearl Inlay Furniture Is Crafted Using Traditional Techniques Dating Back To The Mughal Era. Artisans Source Mother Of Pearl From Mollusks And Embed It Into Wood, Creating Intricate Designs Like Floral Motifs And Geometric Patterns. This Exquisite Furniture Is Highly Prized Domestically And Internationally For Its Beauty And Craftsmanship, With Regions Like Rajasthan And Uttar Pradesh Being Renowned For Their Production. Conservation Efforts Focus On Sustainable Sourcing Practices To Preserve This Cultural Art Form For Future Generations.

Iridescent Sheen.

The most distinct feature is its natural, multi-colored, and shimmering look, often compared to "moonlight trapped in form".

Surprising Durability.

Despite a delicate appearance, MOP has a hardness of 3.5-4.0 on the Mohs scale, making it quite durable and suitable for long-term use with proper care.
